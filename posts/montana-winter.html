Surviving Montana Winter

It's February in Bozeman, which means it's been below zero more days than not. People always ask me how I handle the cold. Honestly? You just… get used to it.

The trick isn't staying warm — it's staying active. If you hunker down inside for five months straight, you'll lose your mind. So you layer up and go outside anyway. Skiing, snowshoeing, or just walking to the coffee shop through crunchy snow.

The upside nobody talks about: the sky on a clear January night at 8,000 feet. Zero light pollution, single-digit temps, and more stars than you knew existed. That alone makes the cold worth it.
